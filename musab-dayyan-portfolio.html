About

My education has been a story of adapting — and that has shaped me more than any single grade. I began at The Educators in the matriculation system, then moved to Karachi Public School in Grade 7 and into the Cambridge system: a new syllabus, a new environment, and an entirely different way of thinking, all at once. That first year was genuinely hard, yet I finished fourth in my class — and third the year after.

I chose to pursue my O and A Levels as a private candidate, taking full ownership of my education: selecting qualified tutors, structuring my own study, and holding myself to a higher standard. Physics became my strongest subject, and Computer Science set my direction.

My aim is to become a computer science engineer — an interest that clicked at a hackathon, watching code my team had written turn into a game people could actually play. I want to spend my life building things with code that solve real problems.
